What we do

Six services.
A scalable supply chain for reuse.

There is so much stuff out there that retains real value through multiple life cycles -- wood, metal, textiles -- but rescuing it has real costs that are better covered at scale. What we accept will expand over time as we create the world we want: Where the latent value in existing objects is fully realized.

Materials Rescue

Working members haul and collect items that didn't get claimed through local listings — before the estate cleanout crew sends them to the scrap yard.

Sorting and QC

Every rescued item gets checked, categorized, and flagged — repairable, rehomeable, or parts. Nothing moves forward until someone's actually looked at it.

Kitting

Orphaned items get grouped into useful sets — the lamp with the shade, fabric scraps with needle and thread, the drill with the bits. Complete kits rehome faster and deliver better value.

Design Lab

Good items don't sell themselves — they need a story. Working members photograph rescued pieces, write listings, and create the marketing that moves inventory.

Preservation

Who loses out when our local unsung history is tossed in the trash? We do. Our working members are trained for an eagle eye for things that might not resell, but have deep social value.

Shipping & Logistics

Not everything sells locally, but that doesn't mean it belongs in the bin. Working members pack, label, and ground-ship items to buyers online — expanding the reuse catchment area.
